Writing Guide: “My relationship with unhealthy food”

Example Blog Post

Introduction
I really like eating chocolate and chips. They taste amazing and make me feel happy for a little while. I know that these foods are not very healthy and that eating too much of them can be bad for my body.

Body / Development
Even though I enjoy these treats, I try not to eat them every day. I limit myself because I understand that too much sugar and fat can harm my health. Instead, I try to eat more fruits and vegetables. I especially like apples, carrots, and spinach, which give me energy and make me feel good. I also drink lots of water to stay healthy.

Conclusion
I have learned that it is okay to enjoy unhealthy food sometimes, but it is important to eat healthy food most of the time. By balancing treats and healthy meals, I feel better and have more energy for school and sports. My goal is to keep enjoying sweets in moderation and focus on eating more nutritious foods every day.

Instructions for Writing Your Own Blog Post

  1. Introduction – Present the topic and mention your favorite unhealthy food. Explain why you like it.
  2. Body / Development – Explain:
    • Why you know the food isn’t healthy
    • How you limit yourself or eat it in moderation
    • Which healthy food you eat more often and why
  3. Conclusion – Summarize your feelings and habits. Suggest what you plan to do in the future.

Tips

  • Use words from the lesson (healthy, unhealthy, vegetables, fruits, chocolate, salmon, garlic…).
  • Each paragraph should have a clear main idea.
  • You can include a personal story or example.
  • Keep your text short and clear (around 5–6 sentences per paragraph).

Guiding Questions

Use these questions to help plan your blog:

  • What is your favorite unhealthy food and why?
  • When do you usually eat it? Often or rarely?
  • Why do you know it isn’t good for you?
  • What healthy food do you eat more often? How does it help you?
  • How do you feel when you limit yourself from eating unhealthy food?
  • What advice would you give to a friend who likes the same unhealthy food?
