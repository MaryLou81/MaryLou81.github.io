Present Perfect – for & since / vs Past Simple

1. Introduction – Why do we need Present Perfect?

We use the Present Perfect to talk about actions and states that started in the past but are connected to the present in some way.

Very often we are not interested in when exactly something happened, but in how long it has lasted or whether we have had the experience.

2. Present Perfect with for and since

We use Present Perfect + for / since to describe:

  • an action or state that started in the past
  • and still continues now (unfinished action / state)

He’s lived in Paris for three years.
(He still lives there now.)

We haven’t seen him since the summer.
(From that time until today.)

The difference between for and since

For – period of time → answers How long?

  • for a week • for a month • for ages • for three hours

Example: She has loved cooking for thirty years.

Since – point in time → answers Since when?

  • since 2012 • since March • since last Tuesday • since the day we met

Example: Since the day I was born, my home has smelled of delicious Indian spices.

3. How do we form Present Perfect?

have / has + past participle

They have owned this café for two years.

I’ve been at the restaurant for half an hour.

We’ve had this dishwasher since February.

She’s known him since her first day at school.

Present Perfect vs Past Simple – Key differences

Present Perfect

We use it when:

  • talking about life experiences up to now
  • talking about duration (how long / since when)
  • the exact time in the past is not mentioned or not important

Have you been to Paris?

I’ve been to this pizzeria. It’s really nice.

She’s worked in the café for ten years.

Past Simple

We use it when:

  • talking about a finished action
  • there is a specific time in the past

I went to the pizzeria last Sunday.

She moved to Britain in 2001.

She opened the restaurant last May.

Example text – both tenses together

My mum is from India, but she moved to Britain in 2001.

She has loved cooking for thirty years.

Since the day I was born, my home has smelled of delicious Indian spices.

But she’s only owned a restaurant for six months.

She opened it last May, and it’s already become very successful.

Rule to remember:

  • Duration or connection to now → Present Perfect
  • Specific time in the past → Past Simple
